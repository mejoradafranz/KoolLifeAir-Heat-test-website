When to Replace vs. Repair Your AC in Florida's Summer Heat

The average age range for an air conditioner is 10 to 15 years, so a system that's reaching that 10-year benchmark will soon need replacing. However, some models can surprise you and operate efficiently longer than expected. When choosing between an AC replacement vs. repair in Apopka, FL, consider these factors.

You Can't Correct a Change in Performance

Through consistent use, you might notice that an older air conditioner lacks the ability to thoroughly cool your living spaces. When you have hot spots or a warm, uncomfortable feeling in your home, a technician can diagnose and fix this issue in a newer AC.

An older unit has suffered too many years of wear and tear damage to properly cool your home. Even with good maintenance, there comes a time when the only solution is a newer model.

You Notice That Your Energy Bills Continue Rising

This is another reason to think about your system's age when choosing between AC replacement vs. repair in Apopka, FL. If you have a newer model and notice a gradual increase in energy costs, a seasonal tune-up will usually fix the problem.

Even when you keep up with tune-ups, your older AC will still lose efficiency. Older and weaker equipment uses more energy to maintain a consistent temperature. In this case, your energy bills and cooling costs will keep rising until you replace your outdated unit.

Your Repairs Exceed the $5,000 Rule

This guideline involves multiplying the cost of repairs by the age of the unit. If the total sum is higher than $5,000, it will be more cost-effective to replace the unit. For example, a repair cost of $600 multiplied by 10 comes to $6,000. In this case, it makes more sense to replace a 10-year-old unit.
