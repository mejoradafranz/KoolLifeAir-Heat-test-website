If you use a heat pump in your home, you're probably grateful for its efficiency when the cold weather arrives. You can maintain the unit's optimal performance by practicing basic heat pump care in Orlando, FL. Winter tips will lead to a more comfortable indoor climate and lower utility bills throughout the season.

Keep Up With Filter Changes

Your air filter does more than remove contaminants from your treated air. By catching particles and trapping them in the fibers, the filter ensures purer air reaches your living spaces.

As the filter becomes clogged with those small contaminants, you might notice reduced airflow and rising heating costs. Change the filter every 30 days to keep heated air flowing efficiently to your living spaces.

Schedule Annual Maintenance

When it comes to heat pump care in Orlando, FL, winter tune-ups are essential. During this service, your HVAC technician will tighten fittings, lubricate the motor, and look for leaks and malfunctions. By reversing the previous season's wear and tear, this type of maintenance will restore the like-new performance of your heat pump.

Check Your Ductwork

If your heat pump doesn't use ductwork, this step is as simple as wiping dust from the indoor air handler's vent cover.

However, if your system does use ductwork, you'll need professional duct cleaning every three years. Wipe dust from the duct vent covers and registers. Keeping these components clean improves airflow and reduces wear and tear stress.

Prepare the Outdoor Compressor

Just as an air conditioner has an outdoor unit, your heat pump also uses an outdoor compressor. Make sure the area is clear. Trim any overgrown grass or foliage near the compressor. Avoid storing bicycles, lawn equipment, or other items close to the unit.

By ensuring the compressor's vents remain unobstructed, you'll protect the compressor from a variety of malfunctions.
