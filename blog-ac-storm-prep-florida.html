How to Prepare Your AC for Florida's Late Summer Storm Season

Home › Blog › How to Prepare Your AC for Florida's Late Summer Storm Season

Even though Florida may not see heavy hurricane activity between May and November, summer storms can still pose a serious threat to your cooling equipment. High winds, heavy rainfall, and flooding can all damage your AC system or affect its cooling ability.

With that in mind, you'll want to follow these tips for AC storm prep in Apopka, FL, in order to plan ahead and protect your air conditioner through the rest of the summer:

Anchor the Compressor

One key aspect of AC storm prep in Apopka, FL, involves making sure forceful winds won't carry your compressor away. Under normal conditions, the fittings that secure the compressor to the concrete pad are sufficient, but taking extra steps for the Florida storm season is a sound precaution. You can either use hurricane straps specifically designed for that purpose, or you can install a metal cage around the pad.

Clear the Area Surrounding the Compressor

AC storm prep in Apopka, FL, also involves thinking about debris that might fly too close to your home. Keep nearby trees and shrubbery trimmed short to keep falling limbs from impacting the compressor, and store lawn furniture, landscaping equipment, and other items in a garage or shed.

Address Potential Flooding

If your property is prone to flooding, take steps to protect your compressor, as flood waters will damage its internal electrical components and leave dirt on its coils. A more involved approach might be to ask your HVAC technician to relocate the compressor altogether, though you can also dig a trench around the pad or take other steps to minimize flooding on your property.

Adjust the Thermostat

Most of the time, HVAC technicians advise keeping your thermostat set anywhere between 70 and 80 degrees. Modifying that setting to keep your home cooler during storm season can help, especially if you lose power during a storm, as the house will stay cooler for longer, but you'll want to watch your energy usage. Keeping your doors and windows shut during rough weather is always a wise choice as well.
