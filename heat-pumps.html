HOW IT WORKS

COOLING & HEATING IN ONE SYSTEM

A heat pump doesn't generate heat by burning fuel — it moves heat from one place to another. In summer it works like a standard AC, pulling heat out of your home and releasing it outside. In winter it reverses direction, extracting heat energy from outdoor air (even when it's cool outside) and bringing it inside. This process is 2–3x more efficient than a gas furnace or electric resistance heater.

❄️

SUMMER COOLING MODE

Acts exactly like a central AC — refrigerant absorbs heat from the air inside your home, carries it to the outdoor unit, and releases it outside. Result: cool, comfortable indoor air.

🔥

WINTER HEATING MODE

Reverses the refrigerant flow — extracts heat energy from cool outdoor air (which still contains heat even at 30°F) and delivers it inside. Far more efficient than burning gas or using electric strips.
