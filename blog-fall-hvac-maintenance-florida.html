Fall HVAC Maintenance Tips for Florida Homeowners

Home › Blog › Fall HVAC Maintenance Tips for Florida Homeowners

Even with the mild fall and winter temperatures in Florida, it's still important to get ready for the heating season ahead. It's also vital to properly care for your AC to prepare for next year. Here are some simple tips for fall HVAC maintenance in Florida that can help you take care of your home.

Change Your Air Filter

Air filters play an important role in keeping dust, pollen, and other contaminants out of your home and your HVAC system. However, they need to be replaced over time as the particles they stop slowly clog them and block airflow. Fall is a great time to change your air filter, or at least check if it's time for a replacement.

Clean Around Your Outdoor Unit

Fall brings plenty of yard debris with it, especially leaves. These can cause trouble for your AC's outdoor unit. Clear a two-foot radius around the unit to prevent debris from getting inside and damaging it. You should also trim any nearby vegetation for the same reason.

Check Your Thermostat Settings

You should check your thermostat settings to make sure you aren't using more energy than needed. As milder fall temperatures arrive, you may find your preferences have changed. Even small adjustments make a big difference. A smart thermostat can also be a great addition, with automated scheduling that lowers your bills.

Schedule a Professional Tune-Up

A professional tune-up is the best way to prepare your home's HVAC system for fall and winter. Experienced technicians will clean, inspect, and fine-tune your system. This lets them catch potential issues before they get out of hand, along with improving efficiency for powerful performance and lower utility costs.

Get Proactive With Fall HVAC Maintenance in Florida

Taking a few simple steps can make a major difference when it comes to fall HVAC maintenance in Florida. You can enjoy powerful heating for the coming months, along with getting off to a good start with a reliable AC in the spring.
