WHAT WE INSTALL

ALL TYPES OF HEATING SYSTEMS

Houston homeowners don't need the heavy-duty heating systems required up north — but you still need reliable heat for our occasional cold snaps. The right system depends on whether you have gas service, your home's size, and your energy priorities. Here's what we install:

🔥

Gas Furnaces

Natural gas furnaces are the most common heating system in Houston. They heat fast, work well in extreme cold snaps, and have lower operating costs where gas rates are favorable. We install both 80% and high-efficiency 96%+ AFUE models from all major brands.

🔄

Heat Pumps

The ideal choice for most Houston homes — one system handles both cooling and heating year-round. Heat pumps are 2–3x more efficient than electric resistance heating and perfectly suited to our mild Gulf Coast winters. Eligible for federal tax credits.

⚡

Electric Air Handlers

Electric air handlers pair with heat pumps to distribute conditioned air through your ductwork. They're also used as standalone backup heat sources. A good option when gas is not available or when going all-electric is the goal.

📦

Package Units

All-in-one packaged systems contain the AC and heat in a single outdoor cabinet — common in Houston homes on slab foundations. Available in gas-electric and all-electric heat pump configurations. Easy to service and replace.

RIGHT-SIZING YOUR SYSTEM

SIZE MATTERS — A LOT

An oversized heating system short-cycles — it heats too fast, shuts off, and never runs long enough to dehumidify or distribute air evenly. An undersized system runs constantly and still can't keep up. Both are expensive mistakes.

We perform a proper Manual J load calculation before recommending any system. This accounts for your home's square footage, ceiling height, insulation levels, window area, and local climate data — not just a rough estimate.
