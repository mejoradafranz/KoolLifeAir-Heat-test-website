COMMON PROBLEMS WE FIX

IS YOUR AC DOING ANY OF THIS?

Houston summers are brutal — temperatures regularly hit 100°F with humidity that makes it feel even hotter. A broken AC isn't just uncomfortable, it's a health risk. We repair all makes and models, usually same day.

🌡️

Not Cooling Enough

Warm air blowing from the vents or the house won't reach the set temperature. Likely causes: low refrigerant, dirty condenser coils, failing compressor, or a refrigerant leak that's slowly depleting over time.

💧

Water Leaking

Clogged condensate drain lines are extremely common in Houston's humid climate. A frozen evaporator coil — often caused by low refrigerant or restricted airflow — will also cause pooling water around the air handler.

🔊

Strange Noises

Banging or clanking usually means loose or broken parts inside the compressor. Squealing points to a worn fan belt or bad bearings. Clicking on startup and shutdown is normal — clicking continuously is not.

🔌

AC Won't Turn On

Could be a tripped circuit breaker, a blown capacitor, a faulty contactor relay, or thermostat wiring issues. Our techs carry capacitors and common electrical parts on every truck for same-trip repairs.

💨

Weak Airflow

Poor airflow from vents means your home takes forever to cool. Often caused by a clogged filter, blocked return air grille, failing blower motor, or collapsed ductwork. We'll find the restriction and fix it.

💸

High Energy Bills

If your electricity bill spikes in summer without explanation, your AC may be working twice as hard due to dirty coils, refrigerant issues, or an aging system losing efficiency. A tune-up can cut costs significantly.

REPAIR OR REPLACE?

WE'LL GIVE YOU AN HONEST ANSWER

Not every broken AC needs to be replaced — and not every broken AC is worth repairing. We give you a straight answer based on your system's age, condition, and repair cost. As a general rule of thumb:

REPAIR IS USUALLY WORTH IT WHEN:
  • Your system is under 10 years old
  • The repair cost is less than 50% of replacement
  • It's a single, straightforward component failure
  • The system runs R-410A refrigerant (not R-22)
REPLACEMENT MAKES SENSE WHEN:
  • Your system is 12–15+ years old
  • You've had multiple repairs in the past 2 years
  • The compressor has failed (most expensive repair)
  • It uses R-22 refrigerant (now phased out and costly)
