HOW IT WORKS

SIMPLE. POWERFUL. SILENT.

UV-C germicidal lights are installed directly inside your air handler unit — the indoor part of your HVAC system where air is conditioned before being distributed through your home. Every time your HVAC runs, all of your home's air passes through the UV-C light field.

UV-C light at the 254nm wavelength disrupts the DNA and RNA of biological organisms — preventing them from reproducing and rendering them harmless. Bacteria, viruses, and mold spores are neutralized before they can reach your living spaces or the people in your home.

A second benefit: the UV light shines continuously on your evaporator coil — the cold, damp metal surface inside your air handler where mold and bacteria love to grow. A clean coil means better efficiency, no musty odors, and cleaner air year-round.

ESPECIALLY IMPORTANT IN HOUSTON

Orlando's high humidity creates the perfect environment for mold growth inside HVAC systems. The dark, damp interior of an air handler can develop mold colonies that spread spores throughout your home every time your system runs. UV lights prevent this at the source — keeping your HVAC clean and your air healthy even in our most humid months.

INSTALLATION PROCESS

QUICK & SIMPLE INSTALL

UV light installation is one of the fastest, most impactful HVAC upgrades we offer. Most installations are complete in under 2 hours with no disruption to your home or ductwork.

01

HVAC INSPECTION

We inspect your existing air handler to determine the optimal UV light placement — typically mounted to shine on the evaporator coil and into the return air plenum.

02

UV LIGHT INSTALLATION

We cut a small access port in the air handler cabinet, mount the UV lamp bracket, and connect to a dedicated low-voltage power supply. No duct modification needed.

03

WIRING & CONTROLS

We wire the system to run continuously (for coil protection) or in tandem with your HVAC fan — depending on your preference and the system type selected.

04

TESTING & VERIFICATION

We verify lamp output with a UV meter, check for proper operation, and walk you through the indicator light and annual bulb replacement schedule before we leave.
